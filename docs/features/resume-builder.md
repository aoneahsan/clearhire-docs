# ClearHire Resume Builder

The ClearHire resume builder is the tool that converts your [structured profile](/features/profile-management) into a polished, exportable CV using multiple professional templates, per-section control, and PDF or DOCX output. Because it reads from your profile rather than a separate document, you tailor each resume to a job by choosing what to show — without retyping your history.

Most of this generation happens client-side in your browser, so building and exporting is fast and works on the [web app](https://clearhire.aoneahsan.com) and the [Android app](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.aoneahsan.clearhire) alike.

## Templates

ClearHire ships several professional templates covering clean single-column layouts and more visual designs. You can switch between them freely; since content lives in your profile, changing templates simply re-renders the same information. A practical rule: choose a simple, single-column template when a role likely uses an applicant tracking system, and reserve more decorative layouts for creative roles where a human reviews first.

## Section control

You decide what appears on each resume:

- **Toggle sections** on or off (hide Awards or Languages when they are not relevant).
- **Reorder sections** so the strongest one leads.
- **Select individual entries** to keep a resume focused and within one to two pages.

This per-job tailoring is the core advantage of building from a profile: one canonical record, many targeted outputs. For a guided first run, see [build your first resume](/getting-started/build-your-first-resume).

## Preview and refine

The live preview shows page breaks, spacing, and length before you export. Use it to confirm dates align, bullets stay concise, and the layout reads cleanly. Refining here is faster than re-exporting repeatedly.

## Export to PDF or DOCX

| Format | Best for | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PDF | Applying to employers and job portals | Preserves exact layout on every device |
| DOCX | Further editing in a word processor | Editable text for you or a recruiter |

For step-by-step export and troubleshooting, read [export your resume as PDF or DOCX](/guides/export-resume-pdf-docx).

## Pair it with the ATS checker

Before applying, run your resume through the [ATS resume checker](/guides/ats-resume-checker). Paste a job description, see how your keywords align, and adjust your wording or [skills](/features/profile-management) where it is honestly accurate. The checker is guidance — it improves alignment but cannot guarantee any specific employer's system will rank you.

## What the resume builder does not do

- **It does not invent your experience.** It formats what you provide; the facts stay yours.
- **It does not guarantee interviews.** A clean, keyword-aligned resume helps, but outcomes depend on the role and competition.
- **It is not a verification of your claims.** A verified badge comes from [employment verification](/features/employment-verification), not from the resume layout.

## FAQ

### Is the resume builder free?
Yes. Like the rest of ClearHire, building and exporting resumes is free, with no subscriptions or per-export charges.

### Can I export to both PDF and DOCX?
Yes. Export as many times as you like in either format. Use PDF to apply and DOCX when the file needs editing.

### Will my resume look the same on every device?
PDF preserves layout consistently. DOCX may render slightly differently depending on the word processor and fonts installed.

### How do I show a verified role on my resume?
First confirm the role via [employment verification](/guides/request-employment-verification). Once verified, that experience entry carries a badge that can be reflected in the app.

### Can I keep multiple tailored resumes?
You tailor output per job from one profile. The fastest workflow is to adjust the template and section selection for each application rather than maintaining many separate documents.
