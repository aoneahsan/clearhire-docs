# Analytics in ClearHire

Analytics in ClearHire is the feature that surfaces measurable signals from your job-search activity — such as profile views, resume downloads, and application progress — so you can see what is happening and adjust your approach. A job search produces a lot of activity that usually disappears; analytics keeps the useful parts visible.

## What you can see

| Metric | What it tells you |
| --- | --- |
| Profile views | How often your [profile](/features/profile-management) is being seen |
| Resume downloads | How many times your resume has been downloaded |
| Application tracking | Where your applications stand across stages |

### Profile views
Profile views indicate visibility. A rising count suggests your profile is reaching more people; a flat count may be a cue to enrich your profile or broaden your [job search](/features/job-search).

Profile views include signed-out visitors, not just people who are logged in. Each visitor is counted **at most once per day** using a per-visitor identifier and a server-side rule, so someone reloading your public profile cannot inflate the number. This makes the count a best-effort, approximate measure of reach — not a precise unique-visitor metric.

### Resume downloads
Resume downloads reflect tangible interest in your CV. Pair this with the [resume builder](/features/resume-builder) and [ATS checker](/guides/ats-resume-checker) to keep the resume people download as strong as possible.

### Application progress
Analytics works hand in hand with [application tracking](/features/applications-tracking): seeing how applications move from applied toward offer helps you spot where you stall and where you convert.

## Turning data into decisions

- **Low profile views?** Strengthen your [profile](/getting-started/create-your-profile) and verify key roles via [employment verification](/features/employment-verification).
- **Downloads but no progress?** Tighten your resume and revisit your [career tools](/features/career-tools) prep.
- **Stalling at a stage?** Focus your effort where applications consistently drop off.

## What analytics does not do

- **It does not identify exactly who viewed you** beyond the signals ClearHire provides.
- **The view count is approximate.** Views are deduplicated per visitor per day on a best-effort basis, not tallied as precise unique visitors.
- **It does not predict offers.** Metrics describe activity; they do not forecast outcomes.
- **It is not a vanity score.** The point is to inform action, not to chase numbers for their own sake.

## FAQ

### Can I see who viewed my profile?
Analytics shows view activity as ClearHire provides it. The focus is on trends and signals you can act on rather than exposing every viewer's identity.

### Are signed-out visitors counted?
Yes. Profile views include anonymous visitors, counted at most once per visitor per day. The count reflects reach without identifying who the visitors are.

### What counts as a resume download?
A download is recorded when your resume file is retrieved. It is a useful proxy for genuine interest in your candidacy.

### How is analytics different from gamification?
[Gamification](/features/gamification) motivates consistent activity with badges and streaks. Analytics measures real outcomes like views and downloads so you can make decisions.

### Does ClearHire use third-party analytics internally?
ClearHire uses analytics and monitoring providers to run the product. See the [privacy summary](/privacy) for the list and what is collected.

### How often should I check my analytics?
Often enough to spot trends — weekly is reasonable for an active search. Use it to decide what to improve next, not to refresh obsessively.
