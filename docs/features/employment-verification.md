# Employment Verification in ClearHire

Employment verification in ClearHire is the process by which a former or current employer confirms that a specific role on your profile is real, after which that role displays a "Verified" badge. It is the feature that sets ClearHire apart from ordinary resume tools: instead of asking employers to take your claims on faith, the platform lets the other party to the employment relationship acknowledge it directly.

Verification is per role, not per person. Confirming one job adds a badge to that one experience entry; other roles remain unverified until they are confirmed too.

## How the request flow works

1. In your [profile](/features/profile-management), open the experience entry you want verified.
2. Start a verification request, identifying the employer or the contact who can confirm the role.
3. The employer receives the request and reviews it on their side.
4. If they confirm, the role earns a **Verified** badge that backs it across ClearHire.

For a candidate-side, step-by-step walkthrough, see [request employment verification](/guides/request-employment-verification).

## The employer side and the timeout

On the company side, an authorized contact can confirm (or decline) the request through [ClearHire for companies](/features/for-companies). Verification requests are not open forever: the employer-side request has a **timeout**. If the request is not actioned within that window, it lapses rather than lingering indefinitely. You can try again, reach out to the employer directly, or choose a different contact who can confirm the role.

This timeout is deliberate. It keeps requests current and avoids a backlog of stale, unanswered asks. It also means a missing badge is not evidence of dishonesty — it may simply mean the request expired or the employer has not responded.

## What the Verified badge means — and does not

The badge is a precise, narrow signal. Be honest about its scope when you rely on it.

| The badge confirms | The badge does NOT confirm |
| --- | --- |
| An employment relationship existed | Your performance or how well you did the job |
| The employer acknowledged the role | Your exact salary or compensation |
| The role on your profile is real | The seniority implied by your title |
| It was confirmed by the other party | Anything about roles you did not verify |

In short: verification proves the relationship, not the rating. Treat it as proof of "this happened," not "this person was excellent."

## Why this matters for trust

Because both [company reviews](/features/company-reviews) and employee reviews in ClearHire are tied to a verified employment link, verification also underpins the platform's review system. Feedback comes from people who genuinely worked together, which is more meaningful than anonymous, unconnected reviews. A verified history strengthens your resume and gives reviews a real basis.

## Practical tips

- **Verify your most important roles first** — the ones a hiring manager will scrutinize.
- **Pick a contact who can actually confirm** the role (HR or a direct manager), so the request does not time out unanswered.
- **Re-request if it lapses.** A timeout is not a rejection; you can send a fresh request.
- **Keep claims honest.** Verification only confirms reality, so describe roles accurately.

## FAQ

### Who can confirm my employment?
An authorized contact at the employer — typically HR or a manager who can vouch that the role existed. They confirm through the company side of ClearHire.

### What happens if my employer never responds?
The request times out and lapses. You can send a new request, choose a different contact, or follow up with the employer outside the app.

### Does a Verified badge mean I was a good employee?
No. It confirms the role existed and was acknowledged. It says nothing about performance, salary, or seniority — see the table above.

### Can I verify a role from a company that no longer exists?
Verification depends on a contact who can confirm the role. If no such contact is reachable, that role may stay unverified, which is not a mark against you.

### Is verification required to use ClearHire?
No. You can build a [profile](/features/profile-management), generate [resumes](/features/resume-builder), and apply to jobs without verifying anything. Verification is an optional trust layer.

### Does verification share my private data with the employer?
It shares only what is necessary to confirm the role between you and the employer you ask. See the [privacy summary](/privacy) for how verification data is handled.
