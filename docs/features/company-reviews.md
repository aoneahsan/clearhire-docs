# Company Reviews in ClearHire

Company reviews in ClearHire are written assessments of employers that are connected to a verified employment relationship, so the people leaving feedback are those who genuinely worked there. The same mechanism runs in both directions: candidates review companies, and companies review employees. Because each review is tied to a [verified employment link](/features/employment-verification), the system favors accountability over anonymous, unconnected opinions.

## Mutual reviews tied to verified employment

The defining idea is mutuality grounded in a real relationship:

- A candidate who worked at a company can review that employer.
- A company can review an employee who worked there.
- Both sides connect to the same verified employment link, so reviews reflect an actual working relationship.

This differs from open review sites where anyone can post about any company. Here, the connection between reviewer and reviewed is the foundation, which raises the signal of each review.

## What you can review

- **As a job seeker:** share your experience of an employer — culture, management, day-to-day reality — to help other candidates.
- **As a company:** provide feedback on an employee tied to a verified role through [ClearHire for companies](/features/for-companies).

Reviews complement your [profile](/features/profile-management) and [verified roles](/features/employment-verification): a verified history plus honest mutual feedback paints a fuller, more trustworthy picture.

## Writing useful reviews

- **Be specific and fair.** Concrete observations help more than vague praise or complaints.
- **Stick to your real experience.** Reviews are tied to a relationship for a reason — keep them honest.
- **Focus on what's useful to the other side.** Future candidates and future employers are the audience.

## What company reviews do not do

- **They are not anonymous free-for-alls.** Reviews are tied to verified employment, not posted by unconnected strangers.
- **They are not a guarantee of accuracy.** A review is one person's honest perspective, not an audited fact.
- **They do not replace verification.** A review reflects opinion; [employment verification](/features/employment-verification) confirms the relationship existed.

## FAQ

### Can anyone review any company?
No. Reviews are tied to a verified employment link, so you review an employer you actually worked for, and a company reviews an employee who actually worked there.

### Are reviews anonymous?
Reviews are grounded in a real working relationship rather than anonymous posting. This accountability is what makes them more meaningful than open, unconnected reviews.

### Can a company review me?
Yes. The system is mutual: companies can leave reviews for employees tied to a verified role, just as you can review companies.

### Does a review affect my verified badge?
No. A [verified badge](/features/employment-verification) confirms the role existed; a review is separate, subjective feedback. One does not change the other.

### What if I disagree with a review about me?
Reviews are individual perspectives tied to a real relationship. Your best response is a strong, [verified](/features/employment-verification) profile and honest reviews of your own, which together present a fuller picture.
