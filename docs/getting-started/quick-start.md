# ClearHire Quick Start: From Sign-Up to Your First Resume

The ClearHire quick start is a short, numbered path that takes you from creating an account to exporting your first resume in one sitting. It assumes nothing beyond a browser or the Android app, and every step here links to a deeper guide if you want more detail. The goal is a working profile and a downloadable CV in well under an hour.

You can follow along in the web app at [clearhire.aoneahsan.com](https://clearhire.aoneahsan.com) or in the [Android app](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.aoneahsan.clearhire). Both share the same account, so progress syncs.

## Step 1: Create your account

1. Open the [web app](https://clearhire.aoneahsan.com) or launch ClearHire on Android.
2. Choose **Sign in with Google** for the fastest setup, or register with an **email address and password**.
3. Confirm you are signed in — your name or avatar appears in the header.

Your account is what stores your profile, resumes, applications, and verification requests, so this step is required before you can save anything.

## Step 2: Complete your profile

Your profile is the single source of truth that feeds every resume you generate. Fill in as much as you can across the seven sections:

1. Add at least one **work experience** entry (title, company, dates, and a few bullet points).
2. Add your **education**.
3. Add **skills** — these power [job search](/features/job-search) filters and the [ATS checker](/guides/ats-resume-checker).
4. Optionally add **projects**, **languages**, **certifications**, and **awards**.

A fuller profile produces a stronger resume and better job matches. See [create your profile](/getting-started/create-your-profile) for what each section is for.

## Step 3: Build and export a resume

1. Open the [resume builder](/features/resume-builder).
2. Pick a professional **template**.
3. Choose which profile **sections** to include and in what order.
4. **Preview** the result.
5. **Export** as **PDF** (for sending to employers) or **DOCX** (for further editing).

Need help choosing a format? Read [export your resume as PDF or DOCX](/guides/export-resume-pdf-docx). For the full walk-through, see [build your first resume](/getting-started/build-your-first-resume).

## Step 4 (optional): Verify a past role

If you want a "Verified" badge on a previous job, open that experience entry and [request employment verification](/guides/request-employment-verification). Your former employer receives the request and can confirm it. The badge does not prove performance — only that the role existed and was acknowledged.

## FAQ

### How long does the quick start take?
Creating an account takes a minute. A useful profile takes 15–30 minutes depending on how much history you add. Exporting a resume after that is near-instant.

### Can I use Google sign-in on both web and Android?
Yes. On the web, sign-in uses a Google popup; on Android it uses the native Google sign-in flow. Both land you on the same ClearHire account.

### Do I have to fill in every profile section?
No. Only the sections you want on a resume matter. Experience and skills are the most useful to complete first; projects, awards, and certifications are optional.

### Will my resume save automatically?
Your profile data is saved to your account. When you export a resume, the file downloads to your device; the underlying profile content stays in ClearHire so you can regenerate it anytime.

### Is the quick start different on Android?
The steps are the same. The Android app uses native sign-in and native file handling for exports, but the profile and resume builder work identically.
