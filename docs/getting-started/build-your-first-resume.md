# Build Your First Resume in ClearHire

Building your first resume in ClearHire means turning your [structured profile](/getting-started/create-your-profile) into a formatted, exportable CV by choosing a template, selecting which sections to show, previewing the result, and downloading it. Because the content already lives in your profile, this step is mostly about presentation: how your existing experience, education, and skills should look on the page for a specific opportunity.

If you have not built a profile yet, do that first — the resume builder has nothing to format without it.

## Step 1: Open the resume builder

Open the [resume builder](/features/resume-builder) from your dashboard. You will see your profile content ready to be arranged, plus a gallery of professional templates.

## Step 2: Pick a template

Choose a template that fits the role and industry. ClearHire offers multiple professional layouts; a clean, single-column design is a safe default for roles that pass through applicant tracking systems, while a more visual layout can suit design or creative roles. You can switch templates later without losing content, since the content comes from your profile.

## Step 3: Choose which sections to include

Not every job needs every section. Use section control to:

1. Toggle sections on or off (for example, hide **Awards** for an entry-level application).
2. Reorder sections so the most relevant one sits near the top.
3. Select which individual entries appear, tailoring the resume to the job.

This per-job tailoring is the main reason a structured profile beats a static document — you adapt the output without rewriting the source.

## Step 4: Preview

Preview the resume to check spacing, page breaks, and length. Aim for clarity: most resumes read best at one to two pages. Confirm that dates line up, bullets are concise, and the most important experience is visible without scrolling.

## Step 5: Export

When you are happy with the layout, export:

- **PDF** — the right choice for sending to employers and uploading to job portals, because it preserves the exact layout everywhere.
- **DOCX** — the right choice when you (or a recruiter) need to edit the text in a word processor afterward.

For format trade-offs and troubleshooting, see [export your resume as PDF or DOCX](/guides/export-resume-pdf-docx).

## Step 6: Check it against a job

Before you apply, run the resume through the [ATS resume checker](/guides/ats-resume-checker). Paste the job description, see how your keywords align, and adjust your [skills](/getting-started/create-your-profile) or bullet wording where it makes honest sense. The checker offers guidance, not a guarantee of passing any specific employer's system.

## Tips for a strong first resume

- **Lead with relevance.** Put the experience that matches the job first.
- **Keep bullets tight.** One line each, focused on results.
- **Match the language of the role.** Mirror real keywords from the job post — without stuffing.
- **Export PDF to apply, DOCX to collaborate.** Pick the format for the task at hand.

## FAQ

### Where does the resume content come from?
Entirely from your [profile](/getting-started/create-your-profile). The builder formats and arranges that content; it does not store separate copies you must maintain.

### Can I make different resumes for different jobs?
Yes. Tailor each one by choosing a template, toggling sections, and selecting entries. The underlying profile stays the same.

### Which export format should I use?
Use PDF to apply (it preserves layout) and DOCX when the file needs further editing. Details are in the [export guide](/guides/export-resume-pdf-docx).

### Does ClearHire write the resume for me?
No. ClearHire formats your real information and offers tools like the [ATS checker](/guides/ats-resume-checker) and [cover-letter generator](/features/career-tools). The facts and wording remain yours, which keeps the resume honest.

### Will switching templates lose my edits?
No. Content is stored in your profile, so changing the template re-renders the same information in a new layout.
