# Using the ATS Resume Checker

The ClearHire ATS resume checker is a tool that compares your resume against a specific job description and shows how well your keywords and content align with what the role asks for. An ATS — applicant tracking system — is the software many employers use to organize and filter applications, and the checker helps you make sure your resume speaks the same language as the job before you apply.

## What an ATS is (and isn't)

An applicant tracking system stores applications and helps recruiters search and filter them, often by keywords drawn from the job description. It is not a single universal robot that "rejects" resumes; different employers configure their systems differently. The practical takeaway is the same either way: a resume that clearly reflects the role's real requirements is easier for both software and humans to recognize as a match.

## How the checker scores

You provide two things — your resume content and the target job description — and the checker evaluates how well they align, focusing on the keywords and skills the role emphasizes. Because it runs client-side as part of ClearHire's [career tools](/features/career-tools), your inputs are processed on your device while you work.

The output points you to gaps: skills or terms the job stresses that your resume underuses, and areas where your wording could mirror the role more closely.

## How to improve your score honestly

1. **Add genuinely matching skills.** If the job asks for a skill you truly have, make sure it is on your [profile](/getting-started/create-your-profile) and resume.
2. **Mirror the role's language** where it is accurate — use the job's real terms instead of synonyms it may not search for.
3. **Quantify outcomes** in your bullets so both software and recruiters see substance.
4. **Cut irrelevant content** that dilutes the match for this specific role.
5. **Re-check after edits** to confirm the alignment improved, then [export](/guides/export-resume-pdf-docx) and apply.

Avoid keyword stuffing. Cramming in terms you cannot back up reads poorly to humans and does not help you in an interview.

## An honest note on guarantees

The checker is **guidance, not a guarantee**. It cannot see inside any specific employer's ATS configuration, and a high score does not promise you will pass screening or land an interview. Use it to make your resume clearer and better aligned — then let your real, [verifiable](/features/employment-verification) experience carry the application.

## FAQ

### Does a high score guarantee I'll pass the employer's ATS?
No. Every employer configures their system differently. The checker improves alignment and clarity; it cannot guarantee any specific outcome.

### Where does the job description come from?
You provide it. Paste the target role's description so the checker can compare it against your resume content.

### Will it write better bullet points for me?
The checker highlights gaps. To draft or refine wording, use the [career tools](/features/career-tools) generators, then review everything for accuracy.

### Is keyword stuffing a good idea to raise my score?
No. Stuffing reads poorly to humans and fails you in interviews. Add only skills and terms you genuinely have.

### Does my data leave my device when I run the checker?
Most career-tool processing runs client-side in your browser. For the full picture of what ClearHire stores, see the [privacy summary](/privacy).
