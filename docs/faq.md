# ClearHire FAQ

This ClearHire FAQ is a collection of plain answers to the questions people most often ask about the app — covering pricing, accounts, verification, privacy, resume formats, platforms, and account deletion. If you want depth on a specific feature, follow the links into the [feature pages](/features/resume-builder) and [guides](/guides/export-resume-pdf-docx).

## Pricing and access

### Is ClearHire free?
Yes. ClearHire is free, with no subscriptions and no in-app purchases. It shows only first-party promotions for the developer's other apps — there are no third-party ad networks. Supporting the project at [aoneahsan.com/payment](https://aoneahsan.com/payment) is optional.

### Do I need an account to use it?
Yes. Because ClearHire saves your profile, resumes, applications, and verification requests, you sign in with Google or email so your data persists and syncs across devices.

### Is it free for companies too?
Yes. Both the job-seeker and the [company](/features/for-companies) sides are free.

## Verification

### How does employment verification work?
You request verification of a specific role from your employer; an authorized contact confirms it; the role then shows a "Verified" badge. See [employment verification](/features/employment-verification) and the [how-to guide](/guides/request-employment-verification).

### Does a Verified badge prove I was a good employee?
No. It confirms the role existed and was acknowledged by the employer. It does not rate performance, confirm salary, or validate seniority.

### What happens if my employer doesn't respond?
The request has a timeout and lapses if unanswered. You can send a new request or choose a different contact — a lapsed request is not a rejection.

## Privacy and data

### What data does ClearHire collect?
It stores your profile, resumes, applications, reviews, and verification data, and uses providers for auth, analytics, and monitoring. See the [privacy summary](/privacy) for specifics.

### Can employers see my data?
You control what you export and send. Verification shares only what is needed with the employer you ask. Companies using [talent search](/features/for-companies) work within ClearHire's matching; you decide what to publish and which resumes to send.

### Is my resume private?
Your profile and resumes are tied to your account. A resume becomes visible to an employer only when you send or upload it. You choose who receives it.

### How do I delete my account?
Use the in-app account-deletion flow in the account or settings area, then confirm. Export your resumes first. Details: [data export and deletion](/guides/data-export-and-deletion).

## Resumes and tools

### PDF or DOCX — which should I use?
Use PDF to apply (it preserves layout) and DOCX when the file needs editing. See [export PDF or DOCX](/guides/export-resume-pdf-docx).

### What are reviews based on?
Reviews are mutual and tied to a verified employment link, so they come from people who actually worked together. See [company reviews](/features/company-reviews).

## Platforms

### Is there an iOS app?
Not yet. iOS is in development. ClearHire currently ships on the web and Android.

### Should I use the web app or the Android app?
Either — they share one account and data. Use the [web app](https://clearhire.aoneahsan.com) on desktop and the [Android app](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.aoneahsan.clearhire) on the go.

### Is the browser extension available?
It is built but not yet published to any browser store. See [browser extension](/features/browser-extension).
